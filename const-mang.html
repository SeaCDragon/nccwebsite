Project Management

Attend regular project meetings throughout the course of design and construction to review the status of deliverables as they are being submitted and to make appropriate recommendations to improve quality and efficiencies. Discuss and record recommendations with client, and work to implement them upon written authorization.

Entitlement Procurement

Work with Client, design team and permit expeditors to prepare permitting strategy for planning and building documents.

Design Management

Work with Client, design and construction teams to ensure programming requirements are adequately conveyed and understood. Assist with interpretation of design and construction documents, models etc., to obtain sign-offs at each stage of the design.

Schedule Management

Prepare and monitor a Project Schedule outlining the tasks necessary to complete the project. Ensure that detailed information is provided by the design and construction teams and incorporated into the management, tracking, and reporting of the project.

Budget Development

Review the Client’s existing budget and provide comments and recommendations. Make revisions to budget upon written authorization from Client.

Quality Control

Conduct regular site visits, including periodic un-announced inspections, as reasonably required to observe the quality and progress of construction. Work with the design team and contractor to implement any corrections that may be necessary.

Cost Control

Review and consolidate all written committed contract values and invoices to-date and prepare a written project cost report. Provide monthly updates reflecting the total committed costs and forecast total cost at completion. Compile and review all written change orders, contractor, consultant and vendor invoices and provide a consolidated monthly payment request which includes copies of the approved invoices, an invoice log, cost report, and a brief project status report.

Project Closeout

Work with the design and construction team to close the project to Client’s instructions, including final punch list and follow-up, equipment training, collection of as-built drawings and equipment operating manuals, O&M documents, final cost report, and coordination of any release of liens necessary.
